length-generalization program in this repo. It collects *methods and concepts* from
**ML, biological sequences, physics/statistical mechanics, formal math / theory of
computation**, and a **measurement toolbox**, all mapped back to the project's three

## Cross-disciplinary synthesis — the same exponents, five vocabularies

The project's β and γ are **not new**: every field has measured these exponents for
decades under different names. The value of this review is that each field also brings
a **mature estimator** or a **conceptual reframing** the project can adopt directly.

1. **β is a critical/correlation exponent.** In physics it is the order-parameter
   correlation-function exponent (η-like) that diverges at criticality (large
   correlation length ξ); in formal languages it separates regular grammars
   (exponential MI decay) from context-free/hierarchical ones (power-law decay,
   Lin & Tegmark 2017); in genomics it is the long-range correlation exponent of DNA
   (Li–Kaneko 1994, Peng 1994); in ML it reappears as the **bipartite mutual-information
   scaling** `I ∼ L^β` (L²M, arXiv:2503.04725). All four are the *same* quantity.

2. **γ is an entropy-rate-convergence exponent.** It equals the **Hilberg / excess-entropy
   exponent** (info theory), the **block-entropy convergence rate** `h_μ(n) = H(n)−H(n−1)`
   (Crutchfield–Feldman; Schmitt–Herzel in genomes), and is bounded by **predictive
   information** (Bialek–Nemenman–Tishby 2001). Important catch (from the toolbox + ML
   reviews): the codebase's existing **Rényi `D_q`** is computed as a per-symbol entropy
   *rate*, i.e. it is a **γ-axis quantity, not a geometric box-counting dimension** —
   recommend relabeling.

3. **Length generalization ≈ finite-size scaling.** The single most useful import.
   Training at context T=512 and evaluating at 2048 is probing a system *below vs above
   its correlation length*; the ratio `r = acc(2048)/acc(512)` is a **finite-size-scaling
   data-collapse** test. Collapse should hold when ξ < T and fail when ξ > T — which
   predicts the *sign* of the project's Mamba-vs-Transformer gap (longer effective ξ →
   better extrapolation) and reframes the refuted edge-of-chaos hypothesis (Binder 1981;
   Fisher–Barber 1972).

4. **α_D = γ/2β is a hyperscaling-style relation.** Physics (RG flow toward the H∞ fixed
   point; emergent length `n*(P) ∝ P^{1/2β}`) and ML scaling theory (Random Hierarchy
   Model, Cagnetta–Wyart; spectral mechanism, Maloney–Roberts–Sully) both supply routes
   to *derive* rather than merely fit it. The **δ (architecture) term** lives in the
   "fast within-horizon learning" assumption of that derivation — so a clean
   Transformer/Mamba/RoPE comparison on identical (β,γ) data is exactly the experiment
   that isolates δ.

5. **"Holographic compression" = logical depth.** Bennett's **logical depth** (and
   Antunes computational depth, and this group's own **epiplexity**, arXiv:2601.03220)
   formalize the project's core intuition: the data are *shallow in Kolmogorov complexity
   but deep in computation time* — low depth for both random and trivial strings, high
   only for organized ones. "Holographic" data = maximizing depth at fixed input length.

6. **Architecture ↔ β capacity is information-theoretically forced.** Fixed-state models
   (Mamba/SSMs/linear attention) vs unbounded attention caches: the **L²M condition**
   lower-bounds required history-state size by the `L^β` MI growth, proving fixed-state
   models cannot match a growing cache on β-structured retrieval (MQAR / "Repeat After
   Me"); both classes remain TC⁰-bounded ("Illusion of State") — a scope guard.

## Honest caveats (flagged by the agents)
- **Power laws can be artifacts.** Zipf/criticality signatures can arise from latent-variable
  mixing (Schwab–Nemenman–Mehta 2014) — run genuine-criticality null tests before claiming
  the emergent strip is "truly critical."
- **β and γ are not fully independent** — two-point MI decay and the predictive-information
  integral are two faces of the same long-range structure; **Hurst is the genuinely
  separate axis** (consistent with this repo's finding |ρ(Hurst, content)| ≈ 0).
- **fGn-vs-fBm category error** is the classic pitfall when relating H, spectral, DFA, and
  correlation exponents — keep noise-vs-motion conventions straight.
- The empirical **edge-of-chaos hypothesis (H1) was refuted** in this repo's own campaign;
  the literature (Bhojanapalli et al.; RASP-L) actually predicts *better* length-gen at
  **high β** (locality/sparsity), consistent with that refutation.

## Round 2 — cross-field deepening (sections 8–12)

**Neuroscience & physiology.** The brain-criticality literature is the closest empirical
analog to the edge-of-chaos hypothesis. Three transfers: **DFA on EEG/MEG** (Linkenkaer-Hansen
2001) as a second Hurst estimator; **neuronal-avalanche / crackling-noise scaling** (Beggs–Plenz
2003; Friedman 2012) with the **exponent-relation test** `(α_dur−1)/(τ−1)=1/σνz` — a built-in
falsifiability check in the spirit of the repo's `verify_predictions.py`; and **Multiscale
Entropy** (Costa–Goldberger–Peng 2002), which by design *peaks* for structured long-range
signals and dips for both random and over-regular ones — a better-shaped "max effective
complexity" probe than the current (monotone) Rényi `D_q`. Strong caution carried over:
single power laws are necessary-not-sufficient for criticality (Touboul–Destexhe 2017;
subsampling bias, Wilting–Priesemann 2018) — demand multiple independent signatures + MLE
power-law fitting (Clauset 2009).

**Econophysics & finance.** The canonical "returns uncorrelated but |returns| long-memory"
dichotomy (Cont 2001) is the empirical precedent for separating **β (correlation)** from
**γ (entropy)** axes — run diagnostics on both the raw series and a magnitude/entropy transform.
**MFDFA singularity-spectrum width Δα** (Kantelhardt 2002) recurs (3rd field to recommend it)
as the single-scalar complexity measure; **DCCA/MF-DCCA** can *measure* the "Hurst is
independent of (β,γ)" claim per scale. Critical methodological import: a **multi-estimator
panel + surrogate/break nulls** (Lo 1991; Granger–Hyung 2004; rough-vol GJR 2018) before
asserting genuine long memory — rough short-memory and structural breaks both *fake* LRD.

**Time-series forecasting.** The **DLinear** result (Zeng et al. 2022) — a linear map beating
transformers — is a cautionary control the repo should adopt (a linear/direct read-out ablation
to separate learned retrieval from autoregressive drift). **β is ARFIMA long-memory in disguise**
(`p(d)∝d^−(β+1)` ⇒ `H=d+½`), and classical theory predicts small-β horizons are *info-limited
but flat* — independently corroborating the repo's **refuted H1** (no edge-of-chaos ridge).
Most actionable untried lever: **patching + a TimesFM-style length curriculum** (dynamic masking
over mixed train lengths) to attack test-length ≫ train-length directly.

**Linguistics & information theory.** Half a century of language statistics already *defines*
γ and β: γ = entropy-rate-convergence/**Hilberg exponent**, β = two-symbol **MI-decay**
(Lin–Tegmark). The cleanest new result: **excess entropy `E=Σ(Hₙ−H∞)` is finite iff γ>1** —
a principled, model-free location for the weak-vs-strong-LRD boundary (and a candidate anchor
for "max effective complexity" near γ≈1). Best estimators: **neural-LM cross-entropy `Lₙ(P)`
at varying context** for γ (Brown 1992 → Cagnetta), and **`‖C(n)‖_op`** for β — cross-checked
via the `P/n^{2β}`, `n^γ Lₙ` collapse.

**Theory derivation + citation verification.** Verified the anchors live; **one correction**:
the RHM is the 5-author PRX 2024 paper (Cagnetta, Petrini, Tomasini, Favero, Wyart,
arXiv:2307.02129); the 2-author *Cagnetta–Wyart* analytic-correlation paper is **arXiv:2406.00048**
(cite both, different roles). α_D source = Cagnetta, **Raventós**, Ganguli, Wyart (arXiv:2602.07488).
**Cleanest derivation:** `n*(P) ∝ P^{1/2β}` comes from matching signal `‖C(n)‖²` to the
`1/P` sample-covariance noise floor — the **factor 2 is the square in a variance-limited
detection comparison**, not a kinetics constant; then `loss ≈ (n*)^{−γ} = P^{−γ/2β}`. **δ lives
in the hidden "fast within-horizon learning" assumption** (the gap between data-resolvable and
model-learned), which is exactly what a Transformer/Mamba/RoPE comparison at fixed (β,γ) isolates.

1. **Re-tool the estimators** (zero training cost): R/S → **DFA/MFDFA**; add **MI-decay β̂** and
   **block/neural-Lₙ γ̂**; relabel Rényi `D_q` as an entropy *rate*; report **MFDFA Δα** and

   architecture's deviation from the data-optimal exponent (Transformer/Mamba/RoPE at fixed data).

   MLE power-law fits + exponent-relation tests before calling anything "critical."
5. **Ground "holographic" in logical depth / epiplexity**; report depth as a first-class stat;
   test whether the emergent strip coincides with peak predictive information / Δα / γ≈1.
